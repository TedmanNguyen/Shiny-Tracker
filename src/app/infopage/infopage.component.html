Shiny Pokemon

Shiny Pokemon are a special variant of a Pokemon. Compared to the regular/typical variant, shiny Pokemon have a different color scheme and may play a "shining" animation at the start of a battle. These Pokemon have identical stats as their regular counterparts, so the only differences between them are cosmetic. Shiny Pokemon were Introduced in Generation II.

The odds of encountering a shiny Pokemon during normal gameplay is extremely low, with a probability of 1/8196 (for generations II-V) or 1/4096 (for generations VI onward). So, obtaining a shiny Pokemon is considered as an achievement by many Pokemon players.

What is Shiny Hunting?

Shiny hunting is when a player uses a special method that increases the chances of encountering a shiny Pokemon. Different methods exist in each Pokemon generation, with some methods reappearing throughout the series. Additionally, the rates for encountering a shiny Pokemon may differ between generations despite using the same method.

Shiny Hunting Methods

Applicable in: Generation II

In Generation II, what makes a Pokemon shiny is determined by something called their Individual Values (IVs). Since IVs can be inherited from parent Pokemon, breeding one shiny Pokemon with another Pokemon increases the chances of obtaining a shiny Pokemon from 1/8196 to 1/64. So, the best (and only) way to increase the odds of getting a shiny Pokemon in Generation II is to breed a shiny Pokemon with another Pokemon.

Applicable in: Generation IV onward

The Masuda Method is one of the most iconic shiny hunting methods in the Pokemon franchise. If two Pokemon from different game languages are bred, the chances of hatching a shiny Pokemon increases. This method was originally created to promote players trading their Pokemon with other players online.

Applicable in: Generation IV, VI, and VIII*

The Poke Radar is used to find Pokemon hiding in tall grass. If a player "chains" the number of successful encounters they get while using the Poke Radar, their chances of encountering a shiny Pokemon increases. This rate increase stops after 40 or more chained encounters.

In Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire, the DexNav is used instead of the Poke Radar. Chaining Pokemon with the DexNav does increase the rates for shiny Pokemon, but the exact mechanics and probability are unknown. Therefore, Shiny Tracker does not have DexNav as a method to choose from when creating a tracker.

*Although Poke Radar exists in Generation VIII, Shiny Tracker only supports games between Generations II and VII.

Applicable in: Generation VI

The concept of chaining also exists in fishing for Generation VI games. Successfully reeling in Pokemon consecutively increases the probability of encountering a shiny Pokemon. This rate increase stops after 20 or more chained encounters.

Applicable in: Generation VII

SOS Battles are when a wild Pokemon calls for help from another Pokemon. If successful, the ally Pokemon will appear in battle alongside the original wild Pokemon. Defeating the ally Pokemon and stalling the encounter causes subsequent ally Pokemon to have a higher chance of being shiny. This rate increase stops after 31 or more chained encounters.

Applicable in: Generation II onward

Also known as shiny resetting, soft resetting is when a player resets the game without saving. Although this method does not increase the rates for a shiny Pokemon, it is used to obtain a shiny variant of a Pokemon encountered in a scripted event. This is because the Pokemon's stats are determined upon encountering the Pokemon, so exiting the game and re-encountering it gives the player another chance to encounter the scripted Pokemon as a shiny.

This method exists for all games that have shiny Pokemon. Because the rates of encountering a shiny don't increase with this method, it is only used in specific situations (ex. getting a Legendary Pokemon as a shiny).

Shiny Charm

Introduced in Pokemon Black 2 and White 2, the Shiny Charm is a key item that increases the chances of obtaining a shiny Pokemon by a flat rate. It is obtained by completing the National Pokedex in the respective game, which requires capturing almost every Pokemon within that game. Since the Shiny Charm provides a flat rate increase, its effect combines with other shiny hunting methods.
